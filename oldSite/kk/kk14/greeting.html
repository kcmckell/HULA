KKXIV

The 14th Annual Kaimana Klassik Ultimate Tournament

The Ultimate Spirit of Aloha

 


As Tournament Director, it is my pleasure to say….Alooohaaa!!! E komo mai (welcome) to the Kaimana Klassik XIV home page. The ultimate ohana (family) in Hawaii has been busy preparing for our 14th annual Kaimana Klassik Tournament. If you’ve ever been to the Kaimana, you know what to expect. We hope and plan to live up to your expectations. If you haven’t been, you are in for an Ultimate treat.

The tournament will be a three day affair. Pool play will be held on Saturday and Sunday, with elimination rounds (quarters, semis, and finals) to be held on Monday. However, the fun, frivolity, festivities, and indulgence will commence on Friday night and continue through the weekend. We have lined up the finest (well, the finest we could afford) caterers, bands, entertainers, beverages, sleeping arrangements, beaches, fields, and outhouses that you could possibly ask for (because even if you asked for more, this is all we can afford).

The Kaimana Klassik Team (organizational staff), presented in the "organizational chart" below, has emphasized Spirit of the Game, geographic representation, size of roster, and anticipated level of play in selecting the teams for this years gala event. We have open division teams representing Oahu, Maui, the Big Island, the U.S. West Coast, the U.S. southeast, the U.S. southwest, the U.S. Mountain Region, two teams from Australia, Vancouver British Columbia, West Europe, and Bavaria!!! Women’s Division representation includes Hawaii, U.S. West Coast, U.S. Southeast, U.S. Mountain Region, and Australia!!! All teams have committed to bringing large squads of incredibly spirited players that love to play and party hard!!!!

It’s not easy selecting the teams to participate in this tournament, especially in the Open Division. We wish we could include everyone, but we obviously cannot. A big Mahalo (thank you) to all who showed an interest/submitted a bid and, if your team wasn’t selected, please try again. Kaimana 15 is not that far away.

Here’s to seeing you in Waimanalo on February 16, 2001.
