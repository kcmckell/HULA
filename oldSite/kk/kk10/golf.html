"1997 Flying Hawaiian Open Disc Golf Tournament"
S ponsored this year by Innova, Disc Golf In Paradise, the Big Island Disc Golf Hui and
the Hawaii Ultimate League Association. In conjuction with the City & County of Honolulu.
The tournament will be held on Oahu and the Big Island. Participants will have the
opportunity of playing at either site or at both. This year there will be individual site
winners and overall winners.

Monday, February 17th, we will be playing at Ho'omaluhia Botanical Garden The map as a GIF file.
Located on the island of Oahu, at the base of the
Koolaus mountains in Kaneohe. This is a new site and would
be considered a beginners to intermediate level course.
This course is very technical and should provide the novice as well as the
experienced disc golfer an opportunity to hone their skills while enjoying
the spectacular view of the Koolau range.

Friday, February 21st, we travel to the island of Hawaii and play an another
newly developed course at Wailoa State park, located next to Hilo Bay Lagoon.
This is Hawaii's longest course with the average hole 320 feet. Bring a
bathing suit and or lots of extra discs, this course has numerous of water
hazards and out of bounds. You may consider coming a day early to get some
practice in for this course.
Golf discs will be available for puirchase if needed

NOTE: This tournament is not PDGA sanctioned.

DIVISIONS::
We are hoping to establish 4 divisions base on registration.

  • Novice Division, (players who have little experience playing disc golf)
  • Youth Division: (12 to 18 years old)
  • Armature Division (intermediate disc golf skills)
  • Wanna Be Division (better than amature but not as good as the big guns)
  • Open Division: (Professional 's)

We hope to have both men's and women's sections for each division. A
division must have a minimum of 6 players registered, other wise Tournament
officers will assign participants to a division. If you are only able to
play one day great, but to be eligible for Site trophies and prizes,
participants must play two rounds. To be eligible for Overall trophies and
prizes participants must play a total of 4 rounds, 2 on each course.
